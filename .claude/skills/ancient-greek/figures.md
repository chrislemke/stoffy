# Major Figures of Ancient Greek Philosophy

## Pre-Socratics (c. 600-450 BCE)

### Thales of Miletus (c. 624-546 BCE)

**School**: Milesian
**Arche**: Water

**Key Ideas**:
- First philosopher in Western tradition
- Sought natural rather than mythological explanations
- "All things are full of gods" (panpsychism?)
- Predicted solar eclipse (585 BCE)

**Significance**: Initiated rational inquiry into nature

---

### Anaximander (c. 610-546 BCE)

**School**: Milesian
**Arche**: The Apeiron (unlimited/indefinite)

**Key Ideas**:
- First to use "apeiron" as cosmic principle
- Cosmological speculation: Earth floats free
- Early evolutionary thought: humans from fish-like creatures
- Justice as cosmic balance

**Fragment**: "Things perish into that from which they came to be, according to necessity; for they pay penalty and retribution to each other for their injustice according to the assessment of Time."

---

### Anaximenes (c. 585-528 BCE)

**School**: Milesian
**Arche**: Air

**Key Ideas**:
- Air as fundamental through rarefaction and condensation
- Soul is air/breath
- Quantitative change explains qualitative difference

---

### Pythagoras (c. 570-495 BCE)

**School**: Pythagorean
**Location**: Croton (Italy)

**Key Ideas**:
- "All is number" – mathematical structure of reality
- Transmigration of souls (metempsychosis)
- Harmony and proportion in cosmos
- Mathematical discoveries (Pythagorean theorem)
- Communal, ascetic lifestyle

**Influence**: Plato's mathematical metaphysics

---

### Heraclitus (c. 535-475 BCE)

**School**: Independent (Ephesus)
**Arche**: Fire

**Key Ideas**:
- Flux: "You cannot step twice into the same river"
- Unity of opposites: "The way up and down is one and the same"
- Logos as cosmic reason governing change
- War as father of all things
- Hidden harmony better than obvious

**Fragments**:
- "This cosmos was not made by gods or men, but always was, is, and will be: an ever-living fire"
- "Character is destiny"

---

### Parmenides (c. 515-450 BCE)

**School**: Eleatic
**Location**: Elea (Italy)

**Key Ideas**:
- Being is one, unchanging, eternal, indivisible
- Change and motion are illusions
- "What is, is; what is not, is not"
- Way of Truth vs. Way of Opinion
- Logic over perception

**Poem**: The goddess reveals the Way of Truth

**Influence**: Shaped all subsequent metaphysics

---

### Zeno of Elea (c. 490-430 BCE)

**School**: Eleatic (Parmenides' student)

**Key Ideas**:
- Paradoxes defending Parmenides
- Achilles and the Tortoise
- The Arrow Paradox
- The Dichotomy (Stadium)
- Plurality paradoxes

**Significance**: Invented dialectical argument; precursor to calculus

---

### Empedocles (c. 494-434 BCE)

**School**: Pluralist
**Location**: Agrigentum (Sicily)

**Key Ideas**:
- Four roots: earth, air, fire, water
- Love (Philia) and Strife (Neikos) as cosmic forces
- Cosmic cycle of unity and separation
- Theory of perception: like knows like
- Natural selection-like process

---

### Anaxagoras (c. 500-428 BCE)

**School**: Pluralist
**Location**: Athens (from Clazomenae)

**Key Ideas**:
- Nous (Mind) as ordering principle
- "Everything contains a portion of everything"
- Seeds (spermata) as basic constituents
- Sun is a hot stone (impiety charge)

**Significance**: First philosopher in Athens; influenced Socrates

---

### Democritus (c. 460-370 BCE)

**School**: Atomist
**Location**: Abdera

**Key Ideas**:
- Atoms and void constitute all reality
- Atoms: indivisible, uncuttable, eternal
- Mechanical causation; no teleology
- Secondary qualities (color, taste) conventional
- Cheerfulness (euthumia) as ethical goal

**Saying**: "By convention sweet, by convention bitter, by convention hot, by convention cold, by convention color: but in reality atoms and void."

---

## Classical Period (c. 450-300 BCE)

### Socrates (469-399 BCE)

**Location**: Athens
**Works**: None (appears in Plato's dialogues)

**Key Ideas**:
- "Know thyself"
- The unexamined life is not worth living
- Virtue is knowledge; vice is ignorance
- No one does wrong willingly
- Elenchus: testing claims through questioning
- Socratic irony: claiming ignorance
- Care for the soul over body/wealth

**Death**: Condemned for impiety and corrupting youth; drank hemlock

**Influence**: Founder of moral philosophy; inspired all Socratic schools

---

### Plato (428-348 BCE)

**School**: Academy (founded 387 BCE)
**Location**: Athens

**Major Dialogues**:
- *Apology*, *Crito*, *Phaedo* (Death of Socrates)
- *Meno*, *Phaedrus*, *Symposium* (Middle period)
- *Republic*, *Parmenides*, *Theaetetus* (Mature)
- *Timaeus*, *Laws* (Late)

**Key Ideas**:
- Theory of Forms: eternal, perfect paradigms
- Allegory of the Cave: education as turning from shadows to truth
- Divided Line: levels of cognition and reality
- Tripartite soul: reason, spirit, appetite
- Philosopher-kings in ideal state
- Eros as ladder to Beauty itself
- Recollection: learning as remembering

**Influence**: Founded Western philosophy; shaped Christianity, metaphysics, epistemology

---

### Aristotle (384-322 BCE)

**School**: Lyceum (founded 335 BCE)
**Location**: Athens (from Stagira)

**Major Works**:
- *Organon* (Logic)
- *Physics*, *Metaphysics*, *De Anima*
- *Nicomachean Ethics*, *Politics*
- *Poetics*, *Rhetoric*

**Key Ideas**:
- Four causes: material, formal, efficient, final
- Hylomorphism: matter + form = substance
- Potentiality and actuality
- Categories of being
- Virtue as mean between extremes
- Eudaimonia: activity of soul in accordance with virtue
- Practical wisdom (phronesis)
- Human as political animal (zoon politikon)
- Unmoved Mover (pure actuality)

**Influence**: Dominated medieval philosophy (Islamic, Jewish, Christian); shaped science, ethics, politics

---

## Hellenistic Period (c. 300 BCE - 200 CE)

### Epicurus (341-270 BCE)

**School**: The Garden (Athens)

**Key Ideas**:
- Atomistic physics (following Democritus)
- Hedonism: pleasure as highest good
- Pleasure = absence of pain (aponia) and mental disturbance (ataraxia)
- Gods exist but don't intervene
- Death is nothing to us (no afterlife)
- Tetrapharmakos: four-part cure
- Classification of desires: natural/necessary, natural/unnecessary, vain
- Friendship as essential to good life

**Letter to Menoeceus**: Summary of ethical teaching

---

### Zeno of Citium (334-262 BCE)

**School**: Stoa (Painted Porch, Athens)

**Key Ideas**:
- Founded Stoicism
- Live according to nature
- Virtue is the only good
- Cosmopolitanism: world citizen
- Logic, physics, ethics as unified system

---

### Chrysippus (279-206 BCE)

**School**: Stoic (third head of the school)

**Key Ideas**:
- Systematized Stoic logic
- Propositional logic
- Fate and determinism
- Compatibility of fate and responsibility
- Extensive writings (none survive complete)

**Saying**: "Without Chrysippus, there would be no Stoa"

---

### Epictetus (c. 50-135 CE)

**School**: Stoic
**Location**: Nicopolis (later Rome)
**Works**: *Discourses*, *Enchiridion* (Handbook)

**Key Ideas**:
- Dichotomy of control: what's up to us vs. not
- Only judgments and choices are in our power
- Events don't disturb; judgments do
- Role ethics: fulfill your role excellently
- Providence and cosmic reason

**Opening of Enchiridion**: "Some things are up to us, and some things are not up to us."

---

### Marcus Aurelius (121-180 CE)

**School**: Stoic
**Position**: Roman Emperor (161-180 CE)
**Work**: *Meditations* (private journal)

**Key Ideas**:
- View from above: see things in cosmic perspective
- Impermanence of all things
- Rational soul as divine spark
- Duty and service to others
- Death as natural process

**Influence**: Most accessible Stoic text; Renaissance and modern inspiration

---

### Pyrrho of Elis (c. 360-270 BCE)

**School**: Skeptic (founder)

**Key Ideas**:
- Suspend judgment on all non-evident matters
- Appearances are all we have
- No dogmatic claims about reality
- Ataraxia through suspension

**Influence**: Founded Pyrrhonian skepticism

---

### Sextus Empiricus (c. 160-210 CE)

**School**: Pyrrhonian Skeptic
**Works**: *Outlines of Pyrrhonism*, *Against the Dogmatists*

**Key Ideas**:
- Ten Modes of suspension
- Equipollence: for every argument, an equal counter-argument
- Skepticism as therapy, not doctrine
- Living by appearances

**Significance**: Main source for ancient skepticism; influenced modern epistemology

---

### Plotinus (204-270 CE)

**School**: Neoplatonist
**Location**: Rome
**Work**: *Enneads* (edited by Porphyry)

**Key Ideas**:
- The One: ineffable source of all
- Emanation: reality flows from the One
- Three hypostases: One, Intellect (Nous), Soul
- Return to the One through contemplation
- Matter as absence of good (privation)
- Mystical union possible

**Influence**: Shaped Christian theology; influenced Islamic and Jewish philosophy
