# Ancient Greek Philosophical Vocabulary

## Metaphysical Terms

| Greek | Transliteration | English | Definition | Usage |
|-------|-----------------|---------|------------|-------|
| ὄν (ὄντος) | on (ontos) | Being | What is; that which exists | "The study of being qua being" (Aristotle) |
| οὐσία | ousia | Substance/essence | Primary being; what a thing fundamentally is | Aristotle's categories; Plato's Forms |
| εἶδος | eidos | Form/species | The intelligible structure; what makes a thing what it is | Platonic Forms; Aristotelian species |
| ἰδέα | idea | Form/idea | Platonic term for the eternal paradigms | "The Form of Beauty" |
| ὕλη | hyle | Matter | The stuff from which things are made | Aristotle's hylomorphism |
| μορφή | morphe | Shape/form | The arrangement of matter | Form + matter = substance |
| ἐνέργεια | energeia | Actuality/activity | Being at work; full realization | The oak is the acorn's energeia |
| ἐντελέχεια | entelecheia | Entelechy | Having one's end within | Self-contained actuality |
| δύναμις | dynamis | Potentiality/power | Capacity to be or do | The acorn's dynamis to become oak |
| ἀρχή | arche | Origin/principle | The source from which things begin | Pre-Socratic search for arche |
| φύσις | physis | Nature | The intrinsic source of change | Physics studies physis |
| λόγος | logos | Reason/word/account | Rational principle; speech; explanation | Stoic logos; Heraclitean logos |
| νοῦς | nous | Mind/intellect | The faculty of rational intuition | Aristotle's active intellect |
| ψυχή | psyche | Soul | The principle of life; animating force | Plato's tripartite soul |
| σῶμα | soma | Body | Physical, material aspect | Soul-body dualism |
| τέλος | telos | End/purpose/goal | That for the sake of which | Final cause |
| αἰτία | aitia | Cause/explanation | The "why" of something | Four causes |
| γένεσις | genesis | Becoming/generation | Process of coming-to-be | Change and becoming |
| φθορά | phthora | Corruption/decay | Process of passing away | Opposite of genesis |
| κίνησις | kinesis | Motion/change | Any kind of change | Four types of kinesis |
| τόπος | topos | Place | Where something is | Aristotle's definition of place |
| χρόνος | chronos | Time | Duration; measure of change | "Number of motion" |
| ἄπειρον | apeiron | The unlimited | Boundless; indefinite | Anaximander's arche |
| πέρας | peras | Limit/boundary | What bounds the unlimited | Form as limit |
| μέγεθος | megethos | Magnitude | Continuous quantity | Mathematical objects |
| ἀριθμός | arithmos | Number | Discrete quantity | Pythagorean metaphysics |
| στοιχεῖον | stoicheion | Element | Basic constituent | Earth, air, fire, water |

## Epistemological Terms

| Greek | Transliteration | English | Definition | Usage |
|-------|-----------------|---------|------------|-------|
| ἐπιστήμη | episteme | Knowledge/science | Certain knowledge of causes | Demonstrative knowledge |
| δόξα | doxa | Opinion/belief | Fallible judgment | Contrasted with episteme |
| ἀλήθεια | aletheia | Truth | Literally "unconcealment" | Correspondence or disclosure |
| ψεῦδος | pseudos | Falsity | What is not the case | Opposite of aletheia |
| αἴσθησις | aisthesis | Perception | Sense experience | Source of empirical knowledge |
| διάνοια | dianoia | Discursive thought | Reasoning step by step | Mathematical thinking |
| νόησις | noesis | Intellectual intuition | Direct rational grasp | Knowledge of Forms |
| εἰκασία | eikasia | Imagination | Grasping images/shadows | Lowest cognitive state |
| πίστις | pistis | Belief/trust | Confident opinion | Knowledge of sensibles |
| ἐμπειρία | empeiria | Experience | Knowledge from repeated perception | Pre-scientific knowledge |
| τέχνη | techne | Craft/art/skill | Productive knowledge | Knowing how to make |
| σοφία | sophia | Wisdom | Highest theoretical knowledge | Knowledge of first causes |
| φρόνησις | phronesis | Practical wisdom | Knowing how to live well | Ethical deliberation |
| ἀνάμνησις | anamnesis | Recollection | Recovery of prenatal knowledge | Platonic learning theory |
| ἐποχή | epoche | Suspension | Withholding assent | Skeptical method |
| φαντασία | phantasia | Impression/presentation | How things appear | Stoic epistemology |
| κατάληψις | katalepsis | Apprehension | Firm grasp of truth | Stoic criterion |

## Ethical Terms

| Greek | Transliteration | English | Definition | Usage |
|-------|-----------------|---------|------------|-------|
| ἀρετή | arete | Virtue/excellence | Being good at what one is | Moral and intellectual virtues |
| εὐδαιμονία | eudaimonia | Happiness/flourishing | Living and doing well | Highest human good |
| κακία | kakia | Vice | Deficiency of virtue | Opposite of arete |
| δικαιοσύνη | dikaiosyne | Justice | Giving each their due | Cardinal virtue |
| ἀνδρεία | andreia | Courage | Proper relation to fear | Mean between cowardice and rashness |
| σωφροσύνη | sophrosyne | Temperance | Moderation; self-control | Mean regarding pleasures |
| φιλία | philia | Friendship/love | Bond of goodwill | Three types (utility, pleasure, virtue) |
| ἔρως | eros | Erotic love | Passionate desire | Platonic ladder of love |
| ἀγάπη | agape | Love | Selfless, unconditional | Later Christian usage |
| ἡδονή | hedone | Pleasure | Positive feeling | Epicurean good |
| λύπη | lype | Pain | Negative feeling | What Epicureans avoid |
| πάθος | pathos | Passion/emotion | Affective response | Stoics: false judgment |
| ἀπάθεια | apatheia | Freedom from passion | Equanimity | Stoic ideal |
| ἀταραξία | ataraxia | Tranquility | Undisturbed peace | Epicurean/Skeptic goal |
| ὁρμή | horme | Impulse | Motivating force | What moves us to act |
| προαίρεσις | prohairesis | Choice/will | Deliberate decision | What's "up to us" |
| βούλησις | boulesis | Wish/rational desire | Desire for the good | Distinct from appetite |
| θυμός | thymos | Spirit | The spirited part | Anger, honor-seeking |
| ἐπιθυμία | epithymia | Appetite | Bodily desire | Food, sex, etc. |
| μεσότης | mesotes | Mean | Middle between extremes | Virtue as mean |
| ἕξις | hexis | Habit/state | Stable disposition | Virtues are hexeis |
| ἐνέργεια | energeia | Activity | Exercise of a capacity | Virtue in action |
| πρᾶξις | praxis | Action | Purposive doing | Object of ethics |
| ποίησις | poiesis | Production | Making something | Craft activity |
| θεωρία | theoria | Contemplation | Pure intellectual activity | Highest human activity |
| βίος | bios | Life/way of life | Manner of living | Three lives (pleasure, politics, contemplation) |

## Logical Terms

| Greek | Transliteration | English | Definition | Usage |
|-------|-----------------|---------|------------|-------|
| συλλογισμός | syllogismos | Syllogism | Deduction from premises | Basic inference form |
| πρότασις | protasis | Premise | Starting proposition | Major/minor premise |
| συμπέρασμα | symperasma | Conclusion | What follows | End of syllogism |
| ὅρος | horos | Term | Subject or predicate | Three terms in syllogism |
| μέσος | mesos | Middle term | Connecting term | Links major and minor |
| ἀπόδειξις | apodeixis | Demonstration | Scientific proof | From first principles |
| ἐπαγωγή | epagoge | Induction | From particulars to universal | How we get first principles |
| ἔλεγχος | elenchos | Refutation | Testing by cross-examination | Socratic method |
| ἀντίφασις | antiphasis | Contradiction | Affirmation and denial | Cannot both be true |
| ἐναντίον | enantion | Contrary | Opposite extremes | Both can be false |
| λῆμμα | lemma | Premise/assumption | What is taken for granted | Supporting proposition |
