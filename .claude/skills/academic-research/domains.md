# Domain Reference Guide

## Philosophy

### Subfields
- **Philosophy of Mind**: Consciousness, qualia, intentionality, mental causation, free will
- **Epistemology**: Knowledge, justification, skepticism, perception
- **Philosophy of Science**: Scientific realism, explanation, causation, reduction
- **Logic**: Modal logic, proof theory, model theory, computability
- **Ethics of AI**: Machine ethics, alignment, moral status of AI

### Key Journals
- *Mind* - Premier analytic philosophy
- *Philosophical Review* - General philosophy
- *Journal of Philosophy* - Broad coverage
- *Noûs* - Metaphysics and epistemology
- *Philosophy of Science* - Philosophy of science
- *Synthese* - Logic and philosophy of science
- *Philosophers' Imprint* - Open access, high quality
- *Erkenntnis* - Epistemology, logic
- *Analysis* - Short papers, analytic philosophy

### Key Venues
- PhilPapers (philpapers.org) - Comprehensive philosophy database
- Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (plato.stanford.edu) - Authoritative overviews

### Search Terms
- Use precise philosophical terminology: "qualia", "intentionality", "supervenience"
- Include major positions: "physicalism", "dualism", "functionalism"
- Reference key thought experiments: "Mary's room", "Chinese room", "zombie argument"

### Influential Figures (Contemporary)
- David Chalmers (consciousness, hard problem)
- Daniel Dennett (consciousness, philosophy of mind)
- Tyler Burge (externalism, perception)
- Timothy Williamson (epistemology, modal logic)
- Kit Fine (metaphysics, logic)

## Neuroscience

### Subfields
- **Cognitive Neuroscience**: Neural basis of cognition, attention, memory, decision-making
- **Computational Neuroscience**: Neural modeling, neural coding, network dynamics
- **Systems Neuroscience**: Sensory systems, motor control, neural circuits
- **Consciousness Studies**: Neural correlates of consciousness (NCC), global workspace

### Key Journals
- *Nature Neuroscience* - High-impact neuroscience
- *Neuron* - Cellular and systems neuroscience
- *Journal of Neuroscience* - Broad neuroscience coverage
- *Cerebral Cortex* - Cortical function and structure
- *NeuroImage* - Neuroimaging methods and findings
- *PNAS* - Broad science, strong neuro section
- *eLife* - Open access, rigorous review
- *PLoS Computational Biology* - Computational approaches

### Key Databases
- PubMed (pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) - Biomedical literature
- bioRxiv (biorxiv.org) - Preprints (not peer-reviewed)

### Search Terms
- Include methodology: "fMRI", "EEG", "single-unit recording", "optogenetics"
- Specify brain regions: "prefrontal cortex", "hippocampus", "anterior cingulate"
- Use standard nomenclature: "neural correlates", "encoding", "decoding"

### Influential Figures (Contemporary)
- Stanislas Dehaene (consciousness, global workspace)
- Giulio Tononi (integrated information theory)
- Karl Friston (free energy principle, predictive coding)
- Nancy Kanwisher (visual cognition, face perception)
- Eve Marder (neural circuits)

## Cognitive Science

### Subfields
- **Perception**: Visual, auditory, multisensory processing
- **Attention**: Selective attention, executive control
- **Memory**: Working memory, episodic memory, semantic memory
- **Decision-Making**: Judgment, reasoning, choice behavior
- **Embodied Cognition**: Sensorimotor grounding, situated cognition
- **Language**: Comprehension, production, acquisition

### Key Journals
- *Cognition* - Broad cognitive science
- *Cognitive Science* - Interdisciplinary
- *Psychological Review* - Theoretical psychology
- *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* - Reviews and perspectives
- *Journal of Experimental Psychology: General* - Experimental work
- *Cognitive Psychology* - Core cognitive processes
- *Psychonomic Bulletin & Review* - Short reports

### Search Terms
- Include paradigms: "Stroop task", "n-back", "change blindness"
- Reference models: "dual-process theory", "global workspace", "predictive processing"
- Specify populations when relevant: "patient studies", "developmental", "individual differences"

### Influential Figures (Contemporary)
- Daniel Kahneman (judgment and decision-making)
- Michael Gazzaniga (split-brain, consciousness)
- Steven Pinker (language, cognition)
- Andy Clark (extended mind, predictive processing)
- Elizabeth Spelke (core knowledge, development)

## Theoretical Computer Science

### Subfields
- **Computability Theory**: Turing machines, decidability, recursive functions, Church-Turing thesis
- **Complexity Theory**: P vs NP, complexity classes, reductions
- **Formal Languages**: Automata, grammars, parsing
- **Logic in CS**: Type theory, proof assistants, program verification
- **Algorithmic Information Theory**: Kolmogorov complexity, randomness
- **AI Foundations**: Learning theory, computational learning theory

### Key Journals & Venues
- *Journal of the ACM* - Premier CS theory
- *SIAM Journal on Computing* - Algorithms and complexity
- *Information and Computation* - Theory of computation
- *Theory of Computing* - Open access theory
- *Logical Methods in Computer Science* - Logic and CS
- *Journal of Machine Learning Research* - ML theory (open access)

### Key Conferences
- STOC (Symposium on Theory of Computing)
- FOCS (Foundations of Computer Science)
- LICS (Logic in Computer Science)
- ICALP (International Colloquium on Automata, Languages and Programming)
- NeurIPS, ICML (for ML theory papers)

### arXiv Categories
- `cs.CC` - Computational Complexity
- `cs.LO` - Logic in Computer Science
- `cs.FL` - Formal Languages and Automata Theory
- `cs.AI` - Artificial Intelligence
- `cs.LG` - Machine Learning
- `cs.IT` - Information Theory
- `math.LO` - Logic (Mathematics)

### Search Terms
- Use precise terminology: "Turing-complete", "undecidable", "NP-hard"
- Reference specific problems: "halting problem", "Post correspondence", "SAT"
- Include complexity classes: "PSPACE", "BQP", "P/poly"

### Influential Figures
- **Historical**: Alan Turing, Alonzo Church, Kurt Gödel, Stephen Kleene
- **Contemporary**: Scott Aaronson (complexity, quantum), Sanjeev Arora (complexity), Avi Wigderson (complexity), Leslie Valiant (learning theory)

## Cross-Domain Topics

### Consciousness Studies
Spans philosophy, neuroscience, and cognitive science:
- Journals: *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, *Consciousness and Cognition*
- Key debates: Hard problem, NCC, global workspace vs IIT
- Search tip: Include both philosophical and empirical terms

### Philosophy of AI / AI Safety
Spans philosophy and CS:
- Venues: *AI & Society*, *Minds and Machines*, *Philosophy & Technology*
- arXiv: `cs.AI`, `cs.CY` (Computers and Society)
- Key topics: Alignment, interpretability, machine consciousness, AI ethics

### Computational Cognitive Science
Spans cognitive science and CS:
- Journals: *Computational Brain & Behavior*, *Topics in Cognitive Science*
- Key approaches: Bayesian models, neural networks, symbolic AI
- Search tip: Include "computational model" or "simulation"
