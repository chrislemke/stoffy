**Key Findings**:

1. **The Poodles Named Atman**: Schopenhauer's succession of poodles were all named "Atman" – the Sanskrit word for "world-soul." This small detail reveals how seriously he took Eastern philosophy and its concepts of universal consciousness.

2. **The Hegel Rivalry**: Schopenhauer's deliberate scheduling of lectures opposite Hegel's was a spectacular failure. The academy chose Hegel overwhelmingly. Schopenhauer spent the rest of his life attacking the "clumsy charlatan."

3. **Late Fame**: Schopenhauer was essentially unknown until his sixties. *The World as Will and Representation* (1818) was ignored for three decades. Only *Parerga und Paralipomena* (1851) brought him recognition.

4. **Wagner Connection**: Wagner was so influenced by Schopenhauer that *Tristan und Isolde* is essentially Schopenhauerian philosophy set to music. Yet Schopenhauer disliked Wagner's music – preferring Rossini!

5. **Freud Anticipation**: Schopenhauer's unconscious, irrational will directly anticipates Freud's id. The emphasis on sexuality as the strongest manifestation of will prefigures libido theory.

**Questions Raised**:
- Is Schopenhauer's pessimism a logical consequence of his metaphysics, or does his temperament drive the philosophy?
- How does the will-to-live relate to Friston's expected free energy (both involve organisms striving to persist)?
- Can Schopenhauer's aesthetic contemplation be understood as a form of predictive error minimization?

## Reflections

### 2025-12-31 - On Will and Contemporary Cognitive Science

Schopenhauer's insight that will precedes intellect – that we rationalize post-hoc what will has already decided – anticipates modern findings about the unconscious origins of decision-making. Libet's experiments, Kahneman's System 1/System 2, and the entire embodied cognition movement echo Schopenhauer's voluntarism.

The body is not just one object among others but our direct access to the thing-in-itself. We experience our body from within as will. This resonates with interoceptive inference – the self is constructed from bodily signals.

### On Pessimism and Suffering

The famous pessimism is often caricatured, but the core argument is rigorous: if will is infinite desire and satisfaction is always temporary, then the structure of existence guarantees suffering. Boredom is the lack of striving; pain is the frustration of striving. There is no third option.

Yet Schopenhauer offers escape routes: aesthetic contemplation (temporary), ascetic denial of will (permanent but rare). Music holds a special place because it represents the will itself, not its objectifications.
