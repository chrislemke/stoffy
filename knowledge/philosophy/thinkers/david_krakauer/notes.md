**Key Findings**:

1. **2024 Publications**: Major year for Krakauer – *The Complex World* (608 pages) plus the 4-volume *Foundational Papers in Complexity Science* (3000+ pages total). A monumental scholarly achievement.

2. **Background**: Born in Hawaii, grew up in Portugal, educated in London and Oxford. Computer science + mathematics + evolutionary biology background = unique interdisciplinary perspective.

3. **"Problem-Solving Matter"**: Krakauer's central research theme – how matter comes to solve problems through evolution and learning. This bridges physics, biology, and cognition.

4. **"Exbodiment"**: Krakauer's term for how intelligence is outsourced into external artifacts (writing, computers, institutions). The opposite of embodiment – intelligence extended beyond the body.

5. **Recognition**: *Wired* magazine's 50 people who will change the world (2012). President of SFI since 2015.

## Reading Notes

### 2025-12-26 - The Complex World

**Key Insight**: The four pillars (evolution, entropy, dynamics, computation) are not just different topics but fundamentally interconnected aspects of complex reality. Evolution requires entropy (thermodynamic arrow of time), dynamics generates the fitness landscape, and computation measures the information content of adaptive strategies. Complexity science is their synthesis.

**Questions Raised**:
- Is emergence epistemological (our inability to predict/reduce) or ontological (genuine novelty)?
- How does complexity science relate to quantum mechanics? Is there a connection between quantum superposition and complex possibility-spaces?
- Can the four pillars be reduced further, or are they truly foundational?
- What are the ethical implications of complexity thinking?

**Connection to My Thinking**:
- The "edge of chaos" is precisely the zone where Nichten (annihilation/negation) operates
- Emergence = Aufhebung = transcendence through negation
- Complex adaptive systems as nodes in graph ontology

### 2025-12-26 - The Four Pillars in Detail

**Evolution**:
- Natural selection as algorithm
- Fitness landscapes (Wright) as possibility-spaces
- Adaptation as information processing
- Connection to potentiality: adaptation = actualization of potential

**Entropy**:
- Shannon: information as uncertainty reduction
- Thermodynamics: systems tend toward equilibrium
- Life: systems that maintain low-entropy states
- Connection to free energy principle: both about maintaining order against entropy

**Dynamics**:
- Nonlinearity: small causes, large effects
- Attractors: stable patterns in state space
- Chaos: deterministic unpredictability
- Connection to edge-dynamics: attractor transitions as edge transformations

**Computation**:
- Kolmogorov complexity: length of minimal description
- Turing machines: limits of computation
- Cellular automata: emergence from simple rules
- Connection to Active Inference: brain as inference engine
