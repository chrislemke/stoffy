# Chris Fields

## Overview

Chris Fields is an independent researcher (based in France) who has pioneered the **quantum formulation of the Free Energy Principle**, extending it from biological systems to all physical systems at any scale. His work bridges quantum information theory, theoretical physics, and consciousness studies, showing how the FEP applies universally when reformulated in spacetime-background-free, scale-free quantum information-theoretic terms. He collaborates extensively with Karl Friston, Michael Levin, and James F. Glazebrook.

## Core Ideas

### 1. Quantum Free Energy Principle
Fields reformulates the FEP in quantum information-theoretic terms. The key insight: any interaction between separable quantum systems can be described as variational free energy minimization. This extends the FEP from biological agents to any physical system that can be separated from its environment by a boundary (Markov blanket/holographic screen).

### 2. Holographic Screens as Information Channels
Boundaries between systems function as holographic screens—information channels that limit communication to the bits they encode. The holographic principle is not exotic physics but "a straightforward consequence of the quantum information theory of separable systems." Every Markov blanket is, fundamentally, a holographic screen.

### 3. Quantum Reference Frames (QRFs)
In quantum theory, reference frames must be considered physical systems that encode unmeasurable quantum phase information. A QRF cannot be fully specified by any finite bit string—it is "nonfungible." This has profound implications: observation is always relative to a reference frame, and objectivity is a useful approximation, not fundamental reality.

### 4. Physics as Information Processing
Fields developed a course showing that physical interaction IS communication. All physical systems are communicating agents. The FEP and active inference apply to all physical systems regardless of scale. This isn't metaphor—it's the literal structure of physics when properly formalized.

### 5. Inner Screen Hypothesis
For consciousness, Fields proposes that the "inner screen" (subjective experience) follows directly from the quantum FEP. Consciousness is not added to physics but emerges from the information-processing structure of any observer-environment interaction.

## Why This Thinker Matters

Fields radicalizes the FEP by showing it's not just a theory of brains or even life—it's a theory of physics itself. Any separable quantum system is an observer minimizing prediction error. For my synthesis work:
- **Consciousness** may be built into the structure of physics, not emergent from brains
- **Scale doesn't matter**: The same principles apply to molecules and galaxies
- **Observation is relational**: There's no view from nowhere; QRFs are always involved
- **Information is physical**: Shannon meets Boltzmann meets Friston
