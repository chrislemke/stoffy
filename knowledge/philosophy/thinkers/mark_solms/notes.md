# Reading Notes on Mark Solms

## The Hidden Spring (2021)

### Chapter Summaries

#### The Core Argument
Solms argues that consciousness is not about representing the external world (perception) but about managing the internal world (homeostasis). The "hidden spring" is the brainstem's affective core—the source from which all conscious experience flows.

### Key Passages

> "Consciousness is our awareness of the slippage between how we expect the world to be and how it actually is."

This connects directly to predictive processing: consciousness is prediction error made felt.

> "The cortex has a secondary role, while the real effort goes on deep in the brainstem."

Radical inversion of standard view. Most consciousness research focuses on cortical correlates (NCC), but Solms shows the brainstem is the necessary substrate.

### Critical Points

1. **Evidence from lesion studies**: Cortical damage leaves consciousness intact; brainstem damage abolishes it
2. **Hydranencephaly cases**: Children born without cortex show affective responses, emotional expressions
3. **Dreaming localization**: Dreams originate from brainstem activation, not cortical processes

## The Hard Problem and FEP (2019)

### Main Claims

1. The "hard problem" asks why physical processes give rise to subjective experience
2. Solms answers: because consciousness IS the subjective aspect of a functional process (homeostasis)
3. Free energy minimization is felt as affect—pleasure when uncertainty decreases, unpleasure when it increases
4. This is **dual-aspect monism**: one process, two aspects (physical and phenomenal)

### Formalization

- Consciousness = subjective aspect of free energy dynamics
- Affect = felt valence of prediction error
- Arousal = centrencephalic gain modulation

### Implications

If consciousness is fundamentally affective (felt), then:
- AI without genuine affect cannot be conscious
- Cortical sophistication is irrelevant without affective core
- Anesthesia works by disrupting brainstem, not cortex

## Neuropsychoanalysis Project

### Freudian Concepts Neuralized

| Freudian Term | Neural Correlate |
|---------------|------------------|
| Id | Brainstem affective systems |
| Ego | Cortical executive functions |
| Drives | Homeostatic imperatives |
| Pleasure principle | Free energy minimization |
| Reality principle | Active inference (action) |

### Why Psychoanalysis Needs Neuroscience

Solms argues psychoanalysis became scientifically marginalized by refusing engagement with neuroscience. Neuropsychoanalysis revives it by grounding Freud's insights in contemporary brain science.
