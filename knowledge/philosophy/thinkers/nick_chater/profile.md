# Thinker Profile: Nick Chater

**Type**: cognitive scientist / behavioral scientist
**Era**: contemporary (b. 1966)
**Institutions**: Warwick Business School (Professor of Behavioural Science)
**Traditions**: Cognitive Science, Behavioral Science, Anti-Depth Psychology

## Core Ideas

### 1. The Flat Mind Thesis
The mind has no hidden depths. There is no subconscious repository of beliefs, desires, or emotions lurking beneath the surface of consciousness. What we experience IS all there is. The sense of mental depth is an illusion—a "conjuring trick" of the brain.

### 2. The Improvised Self
We do not possess stable, pre-existing beliefs, desires, or identities that we access through introspection. Instead, we construct these moment-by-moment, improvising our mental life in real-time. The self is not discovered but invented, again and again.

### 3. Serial Processing
Conscious thought is strictly serial—we can only hold one thought at a time. There is no parallel unconscious processing of complex reasoning. "Multitasking" is rapid switching between tasks, not simultaneous cognition.

### 4. Perception as Foundation
All higher cognition extends from perceptual processes. The same mechanisms that construct visual experience also construct beliefs, emotions, and reasoning. Perceiving is a type of thinking, and all thought is extended perception.

### 5. No Hidden Beliefs
We do not possess stable, stored beliefs that causally govern behavior. Rather, we generate beliefs on the fly when asked about them. Behavior emerges from context and history, not from accessing an inner library of propositions.

### 6. Rejection of the Unconscious
The Freudian unconscious—hidden motivations, repressed desires, suppressed memories—does not exist. What we call "unconscious" is merely automatic, habitual processing. There are no secrets buried in the psyche.

## Relevance to My Thinking

Chater's work provides powerful empirical support for my thesis that "das Ich ist Funktion, nicht Zustand" (the self is function, not state). If the mind is flat and improvised, then:

1. **Voluntas-Kränkung gains traction**: There is no deep self to be free. The fourth humiliation of humanity—that we lack genuine will—is supported by the absence of a substantial inner agent.

2. **Potentiality ontology**: The improvised self aligns with potentiality-first metaphysics. We are not actual beings with fixed properties but bundles of dispositions that actualize moment by moment.

3. **Challenge to phenomenology**: Does the flat mind leave room for genuine transcendence, or is even the experience of opening possibility-space itself improvised?
