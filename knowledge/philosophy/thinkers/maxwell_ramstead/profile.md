# Maxwell J. D. Ramstead

## Overview

Maxwell J. D. Ramstead is a computational neuroscientist and philosopher who serves as **Director of Research at VERSES AI**. His work bridges the Free Energy Principle with phenomenological philosophy, developing what he calls **computational phenomenology**—a framework for modeling first-person experience using Bayesian belief updating. Ramstead is a key collaborator with Karl Friston and has been instrumental in developing the enactive interpretation of active inference.

## Core Ideas

### 1. Enactive Inference: Active Inference IS Enactive Inference
In his influential 2020 paper, Ramstead argues that active inference is not merely compatible with enactivism but IS enactive. The FEP constructs have been systematically misrepresented due to conflation with traditional representationalist frameworks. Properly understood, active inference is enactive—the organism brings about its world through action, not passive perception.

### 2. Computational Phenomenology
Ramstead pioneers the use of active inference to model first-person phenomenology. Core assumption: Bayesian belief updating under the FEP can be employed to model lived experience as described in the phenomenological tradition (Husserl, Merleau-Ponty). This provides a mathematical "translation function" between subjective reports, formal models, and neural mechanisms.

### 3. Multiscale Integration
The Markov blanket formalism allows for multiscale analysis—the same principles apply at cellular, organismic, and social levels. Ramstead develops a "multiscale integrationist" position that transcends the internalism/externalism debate: boundaries exist at multiple nested scales, not just brain/world.

### 4. Husserlian Active Inference
Ramstead applies active inference to model Husserl's phenomenology of time-consciousness:
- **Retention**: Immediate past held in awareness
- **Primal impression**: The present moment
- **Protention**: Anticipation of immediate future
These map onto the temporal structure of predictive processing.

### 5. Multi-Agent Active Inference
Extending to social cognition, Ramstead develops frameworks for shared intentions and collective behavior using active inference. "Shared protentions" explain how groups anticipate together.

## Why This Thinker Matters

Ramstead is crucial for understanding how the FEP relates to philosophical phenomenology. While Friston provides the mathematics and neuroscience, Ramstead shows how this maps onto the rich tradition of phenomenological philosophy. For my synthesis work:
- **FEP and enactivism** are not competitors but complementary
- **Mathematical formalism** can capture phenomenological structures
- **Embodiment** is central to active inference, not incidental
- **Multiscale thinking** dissolves false dichotomies (internal/external, individual/social)
