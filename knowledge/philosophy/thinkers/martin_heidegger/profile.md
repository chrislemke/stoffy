# Thinker Profile: Martin Heidegger

**Type**: Philosopher
**Era**: Contemporary (1889-1976)
**Traditions**: Phenomenology, Existentialism, Hermeneutics

## Core Ideas

- **Dasein (Being-there)**: The distinctively human mode of existence characterized by self-awareness and the capacity to question one's own being. Unlike objects that simply exist, Dasein is always already engaged with its own existence as an issue.

- **Being-in-the-World**: Humans are not isolated subjects confronting an external world but are fundamentally embedded in a context of meaningful relationships, tools, and practices. This unitary structure precedes any subject-object distinction.

- **The Question of Being (Seinsfrage)**: Philosophy has forgotten the fundamental question of what it means for anything to be at all. Heidegger sought to reawaken this question by analyzing how beings become intelligible to us.

- **Authenticity vs. Inauthenticity**: Dasein can exist authentically by owning its finite existence and possibilities, or inauthentically by losing itself in the anonymous "they" (das Man) and everyday preoccupations.

- **Temporality and Finitude**: Being-toward-death is the fundamental structure of Dasein's existence. Confronting our mortality enables authentic existence and reveals time as the horizon within which being becomes meaningful.

- **Aletheia (Unconcealment)**: Truth is not merely correspondence between statements and facts but the primordial disclosure or unconcealment of beings from hiddenness into presence.

## Key Works

| Work | Year | Key Contribution |
|------|------|------------------|
| Being and Time | 1927 | Foundational analysis of Dasein and the question of the meaning of being |
| What is Metaphysics? | 1929 | Exploration of nothingness and anxiety as revealing being |
| Kant and the Problem of Metaphysics | 1929 | Reinterpretation of Kant's first Critique through the lens of ontology |
| The Origin of the Work of Art | 1935 | Art as the disclosure of truth and the strife between world and earth |
| Letter on Humanism | 1947 | Clarification of his relationship to existentialism and humanism |

## Connections to Other Thinkers

- **Edmund Husserl**: Heidegger's teacher and the founder of phenomenology. While deeply influenced by Husserl's method, Heidegger moved away from transcendental phenomenology toward existential-ontological analysis.

- **Soren Kierkegaard**: Precursor whose emphasis on individual existence, anxiety, and authentic selfhood profoundly shaped Heidegger's analysis of Dasein.

- **Friedrich Nietzsche**: Heidegger engaged extensively with Nietzsche, seeing him as the culmination of Western metaphysics and the thinker of the will to power.

- **Hannah Arendt**: His student and romantic partner who developed her own political philosophy while remaining influenced by his phenomenological approach.
