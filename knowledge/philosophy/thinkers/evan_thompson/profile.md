# Evan Thompson

## Overview

Evan Thompson is a philosopher at the University of British Columbia whose work bridges cognitive science, phenomenology, and contemplative traditions (especially Buddhism). He co-authored **The Embodied Mind** (1991) with Francisco Varela and Eleanor Rosch—the founding text of **enactivism**—and later authored **Mind in Life** (2007), which develops the enactive approach in dialogue with biology, dynamic systems theory, and phenomenology. Thompson is a leading voice for **life-mind continuity**: where there is life, there is mind.

## Core Ideas

### 1. Enactivism
Cognition is not the representation of an independent world by an independent mind. Instead, it is the **enactment** or **bringing forth** of a world of significance through embodied action. The world we experience is co-created by our engagement with it, not passively received.

### 2. Life-Mind Continuity
Life and mind share common principles of self-organization. Where there is life, there is cognition (in the minimal sense of sense-making). This is not reductive physicalism—it's recognizing that even the simplest organisms navigate their environments meaningfully.

### 3. Autopoiesis and Sense-Making
Drawing on Maturana and Varela, Thompson emphasizes **autopoiesis** (self-production) as the defining characteristic of life. Autopoietic systems actively maintain themselves and thereby constitute a perspective, a "concern" for their own continuation. This is the origin of meaning.

### 4. Neurophenomenology
Building on Varela's proposal, Thompson advocates **neurophenomenology**: the systematic integration of first-person phenomenological methods with third-person neuroscience. Neither perspective is reducible to the other; they mutually constrain and illuminate.

### 5. 4E Cognition
Cognition is:
- **Embodied**: Dependent on bodily structures and processes
- **Embedded**: Situated in environmental context
- **Enacted**: Arising through agent-world coupling
- **Extended**: Potentially distributed beyond the organism's boundaries

## Intellectual Context

### Influences
- **Francisco Varela**: Autopoiesis, enactivism, neurophenomenology (close collaborator)
- **Maurice Merleau-Ponty**: Embodied phenomenology, motor intentionality

- **Humberto Maturana**: Biology of cognition, autopoiesis

### Influenced
- Enactive cognitive science
- 4E cognition movement

### Tensions With
- **Classical cognitivism**: Rejects representation-based computation

## Why This Thinker Matters

Thompson is essential for understanding the philosophical foundations of enactivism—the tradition that informs much FEP and active inference work. While Friston provides the mathematics, Thompson (following Varela) provides the phenomenological and philosophical grounding. For my synthesis:
- **FEP and enactivism** are complementary (see Ramstead's work)
- **Phenomenology matters**: First-person experience cannot be eliminated
- **Life is cognitive**: Markov blankets and autopoiesis converge
- **Buddhism and cognitive science** can genuinely inform each other
