# Notes on Jakob Hohwy

## Key Concepts to Remember

### Prediction Error Minimization (PEM)
- Brain = hypothesis-testing mechanism
- One mechanism for everything: perception, action, attention, binding
- Minimize difference between predicted and actual sensory inputs
- Model of the world generates predictions tested against reality

### The Hierarchy
- Lower levels: small spatiotemporal scales (edges, local motion)
- Higher levels: larger spatiotemporal scales (objects, persistence)
- Predictions flow DOWN; prediction error flows UP
- Only unexplained residuals propagate upward

### Precision and Attention
- Precision = inverse of noise (1/variance)
- Brain estimates expected precision of signals
- Attention = precision optimization
- High-precision signals get more weight in inference

### Active Inference (Action)
- Two ways to minimize prediction error:
  1. Perceptual inference: update the model
  2. Active inference: change the world
- Action makes the world match predictions
- Perception and action are two sides of same coin
